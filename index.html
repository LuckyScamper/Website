About Evo Corporation

About Evo

Evo Corp is a Star Citizen Organization that deals with helping other groups of players in the Star Citizen universe. It is based around being a lawful organization that does not trample on others (unlike pirates), and instead adheres to the standards set by the Human government in space, also known as the United Empire of Earth (UEE). You can join Evo today in one of the many roles described on this page!

  • Security
  • Colonial Enterprises
  • Mining, Salvaging of resources throughout the Galaxy
  • Research & Development
  • Market Enterprises.

History

Evo Corporation—a name that for years rang alongside a sense of security and reliability on the frontier locales of the United Empire of Earth, and recently in more mainstream corporate and political circles.

A jack of all trades organization, Evo Corporation, or 'EvoCorp,' began in 2942.

It was a typical day on ArcCorp, Stanton; crowds mingled at restaurants and shops, and deals were made by the minute in the many corporate offices towering over the the city-planet.

In a government office, three men, clad in expensive suits tapped their feet. Those acquainted with the business world would recognize (first name?) Turaxis and (first name?) Caligula, wealthy investors with stock in nearly every lawful industry in the empire. Perhaps those who had done their time on an imperial vessel would recognize retired Captain (first name?) Spud, a successful naval officer with many commands and stations under his belt.

"Here you are," said a government worker as he stepped out from the back room. "Evo Corporation, hereby founded under the Office of Business Management, United Empire of Earth; May 12th, 2940. Congratulations."

The Founding of Fleet and Marines

Spud, retired from the UEE after years of service, offered to send some pings out to some old friends. Buddies who served in the Imperial military, fighting Vanduul and pirates. Those years of experience would be crucial in establishing the legitimacy of Evo Corporation as a security firm. Turaxis and Caligula agreed, and asked Spud to send out the messages. They could pay, and they would pay well. Especially for those who served the UEE with such distinction.

Turaxis and Caligula had much grander ambitions beyond Evo Corporation being a security branch, though. Spud was a military man, but Turaxis and Caligula waged their own wars in the cut-throat realm of the corporate sector. Evo would not just be a security firm, they vowed, but also of traders and haulers. The frontier was rife with goods you couldn't get in the core worlds, and Evo needed to tap that market. They would see if their family connections could also be used to hire pilots for the would-be economic division of their corp. In 2942, however, their dreams would start to be realized.

The Foundations of the Economic Division

By 2942, Evo Corp had taken on a number of escorting missions along the trade corridor between Ellis and Terra. Occasionally, the local governments would hire the marines to clean out raider encampments on the surface of their worlds and moons. Evo Corp also provided excellent security for traders looking to sell their goods along the corridor. Turaxis and Caligula hired pilots to help form their economic division, but it was still small and many of the pilots owned Cutlasses at best. Nothing that could haul as the Hulls, Caterpillars and Freelancer Maxes. However, things began to turn when Evo Corp took on a job in the Magnus system. A small company of traders known as Red Sun Shipping made the regular runs between Magnus and Terra. Despite their close proximity, pirates would regularly attack, hurting the profits of the corp. Few of the mercs in Magnus were reliable. They were as likely to take the money and run. Then, Evo Corp began to offer their services.

For a year, Evo Corp were regularly hired by Red Sun for security detail. Evo charged well, their pilots and marines performed excellently, and they refused to abandon a job. It would be completed, or they would die trying. Red Sun offered exclusive contracts to Evo Corp for security detail. “Only Evo Corp could get the job done”, their CEO stated. After two more years, Turaxis and Caligula began further negotiations; a merger of the two corps was on the table. The CEO of Red Sun agreed, and after filing the necessary paperwork at ArcCorp, Red Sun, and all of its assets including the great cargo haulers, became Evo's. Evo Corp now had its economic division.

This development was picked up by other companies, including the fledgling Hurston Seeds, an old agricultural-colonial enterprise created by a Duke of the Messer Dynasty during its rule that was aimed at agricultural development on many planets, beginning with Hurston. After Hurston, its biggest asset, became no longer a viable place for farming, the company struggled. The last CEO decided to sell the company before it would go bankrupt. Evo Corp bought up the company and its R&D department in 2945, incorporating it into its economic division. This led to the idea of creating a self-sustaining colony in uncharted space called “Lamark”, after the Greek mythos, in name of the UEE by the Evo Corp. This colony would use all the features the Evo Corp has, showing Evo’s continued rise to prominence and commitment for efficiency and security. These plans are still in the works in the concept phase, and it is uncertain whether they will be brought to fruition.
